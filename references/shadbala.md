# Shadbala — Six Sources of Planetary Strength (BPHS Ch. 27–28)

Shadbala is the primary BPHS framework for judging whether a planet is strong enough to deliver its promised results. A planet may promise a yoga but fail to produce it if its Shadbala is below the minimum required.

## The Six Balas

### 1. Sthana Bala (Positional Strength)
- **Uccha Bala**: max at exact exaltation degree, zero at debilitation
- **Saptavargiya Bala**: position across 7 divisional charts (D1, D2, D3, D7, D9, D12, D30)
- **Ojha-Yugma Bala**: odd-sign strength for odd planets (Sun, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Rahu); even-sign for even planets
- **Kendra Bala**: angular position strength

| Position | Rupas |
|---|---|
| Kendra (1,4,7,10) | 60 |
| Succedent (2,5,8,11) | 30 |
| Cadent (3,6,9,12) | 15 |

- **Drekkana Bala**: 1st/2nd/3rd drekkana based on gender rule

### 2. Dig Bala (Directional Strength)

Each planet is strongest in one specific house:

| Planet | Strongest house |
|---|---|
| Sun | 10th |
| Moon | 4th |
| Mars | 10th |
| Mercury | 1st |
| Jupiter | 1st |
| Venus | 4th |
| Saturn | 7th |

### 3. Kala Bala (Temporal Strength)

Sub-components based on time of birth:
- **Nathonnatha Bala**: Sun, Jupiter, Venus strong by day; Moon, Mars, Saturn by night; Mercury always
- **Paksha Bala**: Moon strong in bright fortnight (Shukla Paksha); malefics stronger in dark fortnight
- **Tribhaga Bala**: day and night each divided into thirds; each third ruled by a planet
- **Vara Bala**: weekday lord gains extra strength
- **Hora Bala**: planetary hour ruler at birth gains strength
- **Ayana Bala**: Sun's northern/southern course
- **Yuddha Bala**: in planetary war, planet with lower longitude loses strength

### 4. Cheshta Bala (Motional Strength)

| Motion | Rupas |
|---|---|
| Retrograde | 60 (full — treated like exaltation) |
| Stationary (before/after retrograde) | 30 |
| Fast direct | 7.5 |
| Combust | 0 (loses Cheshta Bala) |

Sun and Moon use Ayana/Paksha Bala instead.

### 5. Naisargika Bala (Natural Strength — fixed)

| Planet | Rupas |
|---|---|
| Sun | 60.0 |
| Moon | 51.43 |
| Venus | 42.86 |
| Jupiter | 34.29 |
| Mercury | 25.71 |
| Mars | 17.14 |
| Saturn | 8.57 |

### 6. Drik Bala (Aspectual Strength)

- Full benefic aspect: +15 Rupas
- Full malefic aspect: −15 Rupas
- ¾ aspect: ±11.25; ½ aspect: ±7.5; ¼ aspect: ±3.75

## Minimum Required Shadbala (Rupas)

| Planet | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Sun | 390 |
| Moon | 360 |
| Mars | 300 |
| Mercury | 420 |
| Jupiter | 390 |
| Venus | 330 |
| Saturn | 300 |

A planet below its minimum is considered weak and cannot fully deliver its yogas or significations.

## Ishta / Kashta Phala (BPHS Ch. 28)

- **Ishta Phala** = √(Uccha Bala × Cheshta Bala) — benefic effect score (0–60)
- **Kashta Phala** = √((60 − Uccha Bala) × (60 − Cheshta Bala)) — suffering score (0–60)

High Ishta → planet delivers good results in its period. High Kashta → planet causes suffering.

## Bhava Bala (House Strength)

- Bhavadhipati Bala: strength of the house lord
- Bhava Digbala: house angular position
- Bhava Drishti Bala: benefic aspects add, malefic aspects subtract from the house cusp
